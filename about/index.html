About

Gentle consistency beats heroic struggle.

I’m Deb. I was born and raised in Shandong, China, and I’m now based in Budapest, Hungary. I work in asset management as a data engineer, with a focus on ETF index funds.

Before this role, I worked in fixed-income product strategy, focusing on High Yield Funds and Bank Loan Funds. Working closely with portfolio management teams sharpened my sensitivity to markets and deepened my understanding of portfolio construction.

Earlier in my career, I was a trade financing manager in the petroleum industry. I facilitated petroleum equipment and oilfield service procurement by connecting buyers with financial institutions—primarily insurance companies and commercial banks.

I’ve loved every role I’ve held. Exploring different subsectors of finance has allowed me to see problems from multiple angles, engage with very different people, and learn distinct ways of thinking. These experiences and skills have shaped who I am today.

I’m not a big fan of traveling for its own sake, yet work has taken me across much of Europe and Asia. I’ve experienced things I could never have imagined at birth—privileges I don’t take lightly. The journey continues, and I don’t plan on settling down anytime soon. I consider myself a citizen of the world.

I deeply cherish my family and friends. The person who has influenced me the most is my grandmother. She taught me optimism, diligence, how to enjoy the process, and not to obsess over outcomes. She shaped my early way of thinking and continues to influence how I live and work today. I love and care for her deeply.

I’m a devoted long-sleeper, though I’ll happily wake up early for a limited-edition pistachio croissant. I’m currently building the habit of rising earlier, hoping to make better use of quiet mornings for exercise, reading, and writing.

I read to pass the time—nonfiction when I’m calm, fiction when I’m inspired. I’m halfway into the world of science fiction. Through this website, I plan to write more thoughtful book reviews, using writing as a way to read more broadly and deeply.

I’m highly skilled in Northern Chinese cuisine and fairly good at baking. My most-requested dish is tea eggs. Cooking calms me; it’s another form of meditation.
